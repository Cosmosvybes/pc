# Marketing Strategy: Reddit (r/Parenting)

**Goal:** Secure your first 100 users by providing genuine value, avoiding "spam" bans, and leveraging the free trial "Hook".

## 1. The "Vulnerable Parent" Strategy
Reddit hates ads. Reddit loves stories.
You must position yourself not as a "CEO selling a product," but as a **Fellow Parent solving a personal pain.**

**Your Persona:**
*   You are transparent about your struggles (anger, overwhelm).
*   You are sharing a "hack" or a tool you made for yourself first.
*   The "App" is just the easiest way to share that tool.

## 3. The Follow-Up (Comments)
*   **Reply to everyone.**
*   **Be humble.** If they say "This is expensive," say: "I appreciate that feedback! I'm just trying to cover the AI costs, but I tried to make the free trial accessible to everyone."
*   **Ask for features.** "What situation do you struggle with most? I can add it to the script library."

## 4. Other Communities
Once you post on `r/Parenting`, wait 24 hours, then crosspost (adjusting the tone slightly) to:
*   `r/daddit` (Very supportive, loves "Dad builds tool" stories).
*   `r/toddlers` (The core target audience).
